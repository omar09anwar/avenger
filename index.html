What Is Pollution?

Pollution is the introduction of harmful materials into the environment. Landfills collect
garbage and other land pollution in a central location. Many places are running out of
space for landfills.
















◀

Types Of Pollution

  • Air Pollution
  • Water Pollution
  • Soil Pollution
  • Noise Pollution
  • Light Pollution










    • ◀

    Air Pollution:

    Air pollution is the release of gases and small fragments of human-made particles and chemicals into the air, the concentration of which is harmful to all living and breathing creatures on this planet. Upon studying global warming, researchers have determined that air pollution is a significant contributor to this problem.





    ◀

    Water Pollution:

    Water is one of the basic necessities of life. Though about three-fourths of the Earth surface covers with water bodies, but the availability of fresh water to us is of a serious issue. Water pollution is another main type of pollution. It gets polluted when harmful substances enter the water bodies or seep down the water table. It has the unique feature of dissolving many substances. Dissolved gases like oxygen are essential for aquatic life.





    ◀

    Soil Pollution:

    Among the many other types of pollution, soil pollution or land pollution is the next one. Soil is one of the important natural resources. The soil provides shelter to many animals and supports the growth of plants from which we obtain our basic needs. Soil also gets harmful and polluted when some harmful substances mix with the soil.





    ◀

    Noise Pollution:

    Noise is an unwanted sound in our environment. This causes adverse effects on humans. It has penetrated deep in the modern life due to development of industry, machinery and technologies. Noise disturbs the tranquillity of human life. Loud sounds and noises are caused in factories, roads, by aeroplanes or in marketplaces. Sound intensities are measured in the unit named as decibels (dB). For our system, sound beyond 80 dB causes noise pollution.





    ◀

    Light Pollution:

    This pollution occurs from the unnecessary light in our surroundings. Unnecessary lights and using more energy to light up instead of using minimum light energy for the work need to be done cause light pollution.

    Cuases Of Pollution

  • Air Pollution

    • ◀

    Cuase Of Air Pollution:

  • The combustion of fossil fuels, biomass, automobile exhaust and many others have released carbon dioxide, carbon mono oxide, sulphur dioxide and also many other components of toxic gases in the air.
  • Some of the sources of air pollution are natural and some are made of pollutants. Forest fires, volcanic eruption, dust storms, decaying organic matters are the causes of air pollution from the natural sources. On the other hand, auto mobiles, agriculture, power plants industries etc are the man-made sources that cause air pollution to exceed.








    • ◀

    Cuase Of Water Pollution:

  • Water gets polluted from different sources like domestic sources which include detergents and sewage, agricultural wastes which include chemicals like fertilizers and pesticides, solid wastes and industrial wastes which contain toxic substances like dyes, grease, acids, oils, etc.
  • Water pollution causes serious health issues and also an environmental problem. Organic wastes cause water-borne diseases like dysentery, malaria, etc. Inorganic chemicals affect the kidney, reproductive system and respiratory system of humans; harm the aquatic plants and animals; leads to decrease in crop production.








    • ◀

    Cuase Of Soil Pollution

  • There are many pollutants which lead to soil pollution. Excessive use of fertilizers pollutes the soil. The nitrogen compound from the soil makes it unfit for the plants to derive nutrients.
  • Industrial wastewater contains harmful chemicals which enter the soil with wastewater and make the soil infertile.








    • ◀

    Cuase Of Noise Pollution

  • Man-made sources like horns of vehicles, auto mobiles, aeroplanes, speakers, sirens, loudspeakers and even shouting causes noise pollution. A natural source like thundering causes some sort of noise pollution.
  • Aircraft causes more discomfort than road noises. Air traffic has increased a much higher level. Loud noise producing high jet aircraft’s that not only disturb human communication but also damage hearing permanently.








    • ◀

    Cuase Of Light Pollution

  • Brightening of sky during nights makes the star and planets visible by improper lighting. This affects the observation of astronomical phenomena and making it difficult to see.
  • The street lamps are lined up one after the other increases the amount of light energy.















    • ◀

    How To Prevent Pollution

  • Air Pollution
  • Water Pollution
  • Soil Pollution
  • Noise Pollution
  • Light Pollution










    • Prevent Air Pollution:

    Using solar energy and CNG in place of polluting fuels, checking the auto mobiles regularly, providing the chimneys of the factories with appropriate filters are the measures in order to control the particulate matter or other pollutants in the air.











    Prevent Water Pollution:

    This pollution can be controlled by using separate sewage tanks for individual houses, minimizing the use of detergent, treating the industrial or domestic wastes before discharging, setting wastewater treatment plants and many more.











    Prevent Soil Pollution:

    Proper disposal of solid wastes needs to be done in sanitary landfills. Discharging industrial waste without proper treatment must not be done. Planting more trees won’t lead to soil erosion. More organic fertilizers need to be used instead of chemical fertilizers or manures.











    Prevent Noise Pollution:

    To reduce noise pollution, we need to take certain actions. Use of loudspeakers during the late night and early morning must not be done and also maintain the low sound intensity during any time of the day. Unnecessary use of horns should be restricted. Vehicles should use proper silencers. The noisy vehicles should not be allowed to enter the residential premises. Thick glass panes in the windows cut down the noise pollution to a larger extent. Trees absorb noises, so planting trees help to reduce the extent of noise pollution.











    Prevent Light Pollution:

    Light needs to be minimally used, while making sure the streets and roads are lit, low volts of light can be a preferred choice.
